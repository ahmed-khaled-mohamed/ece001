In a broad sense, the term Internet of Things includes everything connected to the Internet, but is increasingly used to define things that "talk" to each other. "Simply, the Internet of Things consists of devices - from simple sensors to smartphones and wearable devices," says Matthew Evans, head of the IoT program at techUK. "To help someone with a certain task or learn from a process. In fact, this ranges from smart mirrors to lighthouses in and out of stores. "It is about networks, devices, and data," explains Caroline Gorski, head of Internet of Things at Digital Catapult. The Internet of Things allows devices on closed private Internet connections to communicate with others and "The Internet of Things brings these networks together. It gives devices the opportunity to connect not only to nearby silos but across different network types and create a much more connected world.
